# VelNit Brain Decisions

A running log of decisions made about the VelNit Brain itself, and about
systems (like the Content Operating System) built directly against it.
Each entry: what changed, why, who approved it, and its impact.

---

### 2026-07 - Build the Content Operating System as a narrow, review-first MVP

**What changed.** Rather than waiting for the Brain to be fully populated
(all 13 core concepts fully worked, a large library of tagged articles and
prompts), the founder chose to build the first version of the Content
Operating System now, against exactly what exists today: VRIF, TALK,
Writing DNA, and the Knowledge Graph's schema plus two worked concepts.

**Why it changed.** The Knowledge Graph document's own closing section
("Applying the Principle: Can the Brain Already Support This?") concluded
the Brain is "not yet, fully - but close enough to start," and that the
right next step is proving the workflow end-to-end on one real topic
rather than writing another foundational document first.

**Who approved it.** Founder (Babita).

**Impact.**
- Product: adds a founder-only `/studio` workspace - Content OS Phase B -
  see `supabase/migrations/0002_content_os.sql` and `lib/ai/`.
- AI: generation is grounded in the full text of all four Brain documents
  (compiled into `lib/ai/brain-content.generated.ts` by
  `npm run generate:brain`), not a hand-written paraphrase - so the system
  prompt drifts only when `brain/*.md` is deliberately edited.
- Content: nothing generated by the Content OS publishes automatically.
  Every draft sits in `draft` or `needs_revision` status until a founder
  explicitly approves it (see `content_items.status` and the review
  checklist in `lib/ai/review-checklist.ts`).
- Knowledge Graph: this is the first real test of whether "one article ->
  five concepts, ten prompts, three workshops" (Section 6) holds up in
  practice. The pilot topic - "Loneliness after the children leave home" -
  was deliberately chosen because Loneliness is one of the two concepts
  already fully worked through the 8-question schema.

**Open item carried forward.** The pilot brief's VRIF pillar labels
("Couple Connection", "Self Connection", "Community Connection") do not
match VRIF's six canonical pillar names (Presence, Attunement, Expression,
Reciprocity, Shared Meaning, Adaptability). Resolved pragmatically for
Phase B: `content_briefs.vrif_pillars` is a free-text array, not an enum,
so the founder's literal labels are stored as given. A future Brain
revision should either fold these into the canonical six or formally
register them as a second, complementary taxonomy - not silently drift
into two incompatible pillar vocabularies.
